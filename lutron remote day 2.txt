PDUs on ESNs:
T-Series: +30 PDUs
EcoSystem: +30 PDUs
Softswitch / 0-10V: +14 PDUs
Phase Adaptive: +0 PDUs
DALI: +0 PDUs


DALI drivers MAY BE polarity sensitive --- some drivers are polarity sensitive -- NOT STANDARD
ECOSYSTEM IS NOT polarity sensitive --- we have control over all the drivers, so they are standard


--- ecosystem ESN ----

loop1 and loop2 commonly miswired bc loop2 is on the LEFT
STRANDED WIRE DOES NOT PLUG INTO THE TERMINALS -- use SOLID WIRE
EcoSystem is DIGITAL ONLY -- we do NOT PROVIDE POWER FROM THE ESN to the LIGHTS- -- they have to get their power from elsewhere
the power coming in on the ESN only powers the unit



--- DALI ESN ---

USES THE SAME DALI DIN MODULE but is put inside the ESN enclosure
NO wired sensor inputs
2 DALI loops
DOES NOT provide power


--- LED+ Phase Adaptive ESN ---

SAME DIN MODULE in ESN enclosure



--- Troubleshooting ---

Some contractors accidentally take out the emergency mode jumper -- THIS will put the unit into emergency mode -- it may also be LOOSE

Make sure you are not drawing too many PDUs or backfeeding PDUs -- unit will not function

you can see whether MUX and MUX(BAR) are reversed ---> units will show an LED blink code that the unit is not communicating properly over the QS-Link (reverse waterfall pattern - lights move upwards in a pattern)

should NOT have Line voltage on the QS-Link

you can see if the unit works without being connected with the QS-Link -- then u know something is wrong with the QS-Link



ESN Switching issues:
--
output of one relay is feeding another
power is being back fed -- an alternate source of power feeding the lights
ESN is not powered correctly


if you DO NOT see the LINE VOLTAGE SHIELD on an ESN - you need to notify the EC -- it is part of the unit

wires in the line and low voltage areas SHOULD BE ISOLATED - notify EC

on ESNs, hots can be jumpered to keep the loads always on

WAYGO connector - type of multi-wire jumper box

static shock shield present on low voltage section of the ESN -- isolates any ESD from the circuit board, also provides a backup shield against a high voltage lead that is accidentally touching a trace on the board


reset button on the board acts as a soft power cycle

Out of Box the unit -> triple tap, hold, triple tap the PROGRAM button -- factory reset to out of the box settings -- it also erases all programming (have to reprogram if standalone) -- if connected to a processor, it can get the database back from the processor

RED LED on LOOP1/2
