--- Wallstation troubleshooting ---


mux voltage --> one of them should be positive (+V MUX) one should be negative (-V MUX-BAR)

some pages on the support wiki explaining the voltage of mux
mux should be 1 voltage, mux-bar should be ABOUT HALF of that

this applies to any device on the QS Link

the devices are designed to work between 18 and 32 V

Interfaces

--- QSE-CI-DMX ---

program button is used to activate / identify / OOB (3tap / hold / 3tap)

the dip switches VARY DEPENDING ON WHAT FIRMWARE YOU HAVE -- different for standalone

--- NWK ---

the dip switches are ONLY applicable to the RS232 connection
the way the baud rate diagram is printed expects you to ROLL the unit toward you --- NOT to flip the unit around and look at it from the front
only way to OOB the unit is to connect it to one of the RJ45 or RS232 and send a command from the software


--- IO / QSE-IO ---

in standalone, the outputs follow the inputs
in athena / quantum - you can configure how the inputs and outputs are controlled
if you need to use the outputs, you have to tie the commons together externally to the unit (???)


--- QSM ---

pre-wire -- ask the contractor to mark the sensor wires coming into the QSM -- if they don't mark them, you have no idea which wire goes to which sensor when trying to troubleshoot

same process to OOB -- 3tap/hold/3tap button on front

Sensors
----

--- Occ / Vac sensors ---

wired version is ONLY occ sensor

hole allows you to use a laser to activate the sensor to a QSM -- tag twice - once to wake it up - once to send out its serial number
you have to put the QSM into activation mode to activate sensors to it

3 gray buttons on the back of the sensor - used to control various functions printed on the back of the unit -- press and hold for 6 sec then click to change, hold again to lock in the setting

you can only get timeouts less than 15 minutes by using the gray button on the back for setting the time

WALL mounted OCC sensors
---
Wall / corner / hallway versions -- they all look basically the same
the only way to know the difference is looking at the model number on the back
the difference between them is:
wall has a 180deg view - 50 ft out
corner has a 90deg view - 50 ft out
hallway sensor only has a 10-20deg view, but goes 150ft out -- designed to be at the end of a hallway and look down the hallway
has a laser hole on the bottom

--- WIRED OCC sensors ---

some may have ultrasonic on both sides - just changes coverage range
you pop off the dome to access the dip switches
always set B3&4 dip switches to the on position

red trim - IR
green trim - ultrasonic
black trim - timer - set to fully clockwise, fully counterclockwise to put into test mode

DO NOT force the trim pots too hard -- they can break and will have to replace the whole sensor

relay model will have a BLUE trim pot inside -- we do not use the actual trim pot but it can be used to identify what model it is without removing unit --- make sure it is kept FULLY clockwise

relay models are very commonly MISWIRED


--- Wireless daylight sensor ---

it can rotate WHILE it is mounted in the bracket, you can click rotate it into the direction it needs to be facing

make sure the battery is put in the RIGHT direction -- + has to be down toward the circuit board.  they are NOT pre-installed, the contractor has to install them


--- Wired daylight sensor ---
wiring:
yellow wire is for the sensor signal / white gets capped off

streams its data constantly

signal goes from about 0-11V depending on how much light is detected

can use the multimeter to check the voltage between common and the signal wire




--- LUTRON SHADES ---

lighting systems get sold through distribution, shades are sold directly to a shade dealer

when troubleshooting - need to isolate issue owner between EC/Shade Dealer on punch list

expectations:
shades on the BOM are in the DB/Oneline
provided the contact info for the shade dealer
scheduled for enough service time to program shades
startup checklist complete
know the players onsite

ESCALATE ALL SHADE ISSUES THROUGH THE PROJECT MANAGER -- DO NOT BRING UP WITH THE EC OR GC


--- Shade Components ---

Header - top of the window
Mullion - vertical bar in the window
Sill - bottom of the window

Roller shades - 95% of the time

Bracket - attaches to the building - holds the shade up
Tube with fabric - aluminum tube the fabric attached to
Fabric
EDU (electronic drive unit) - motor for the shade
Hembar - bar at the bottom of the shade

Sivoia QS - family of roller models -- premium whisper quiet - ultra reliable

Contract Roller - family of roller models -- more cost effective - not whisper quiet

Shades are a QS device but DO NOT get power from the QS link - they need their own power supply

exposed hembar with wool pile used for blackout shades -- the wool pile blocks the light

coupled shades -- 1 of the shades has a drive, and the other ones are just coupled and are controlled by the one with the drive -- reduces cost

coupled roller shades can have a maximum of 6 panels driven by 1 motor

angled coupled roller can have up to 3 panels -- maximum of 45 degree angle

mounting - naked mount - fascia - pocket mount (common)
