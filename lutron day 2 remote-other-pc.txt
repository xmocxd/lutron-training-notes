PDU = power that goes out on the QS-Link that powers devices (seeTouch keypads [NOT picos] / QSMs / NWK-IO or DMX devices), does NOT go to the lights



--- QP6 (athena 1 link proc) ---

line voltage cover must be on, should be written up in trip report if not

processor takes about 30-40 sec to boot up

class 2 connection = low voltage

if any line voltage connections made outside the line voltage cover / in the low voltage area, must be reported / fixed



--- Athena Gateway ---

looks identical to a wireless proc
counts as 1 processor count**

can tell apart from a QSM because the QSM has 3 dots instead of 2

mac address and serial number on the back -- need to physically activate the device

ethernet port also on the back -- powers over POE and connects to network

should be discoverable on the network

can have a surface mount bracket or a recess mount bracket -- can disconnect by giving a quarter turn
same bracket used for QSM, Gateway, A-RF2


LED blink green -- normal operation
Blue blink -- downloading firmware --- may download auto update of firmware when connected to the cloud (usually at initial connection?)
Red - issue




--- LTE Modem (Digi modem) ---

included with most systems as a line item
provides connection over LTE / cellular
the purpose of this is for startup -- to be a connected system during startup
usually on startup they dont have the building connectivity or internet set up

up to 16 processors can be connected

can be either powered by POE or plug in power supply -- you HAVE to use a power injector -- power injector does 18V - our switch does 48V -- device cant handle 48V
also comes with battery pack

you want the contractor to have this up and running before you ever come to the job site

digi dashboard -- allows you to remotely set up the digi modem

we require a minimum of 3 bars of service -- might need to be mounted outside the electrical closet / maybe near a window
the battery pack allows you to walk the modem around the building to find service and mount it in that location

when you mark the service is complete, we can go in and discontinue the cellular service

pre-visit checks can be done through the dashboard

will ONLY use DHCP - cannot be used with static IPs

port 1 LAN connection - is the only port we use -- CAT 5e minimum --- 328ft maximum

can actually have up to 2 sim cards providing service


CAN be directly plugged into the athena processor, OR can be plugged into a switch and then hooked to the processor


contractor needs to send a picture of the label with mac address of the modem to you


BLINK CODES (LTE LED):
Solid Yellow - initializing
Flashing (any color) - connecting
Solid Green - connected via 2G/3G
Solid Blue - connected via 4G LTE
Alternating Red/Yellow - Upgrading firmware (do not power off)



some customers get their own sim card and keep the modem as the main connectivity for the athena system.  this allows them to isolate the system from their building network, etc.

30 days after startup, we disable cell service to the unit

--- DIN MODULES ---



QSN-4A5-D - Phase Adaptive Power Modules
---
4 zone power module

one load type per zone

NOT rated for switching loads

LEDs:
Power - continuous on - normal
Power - Off - no power
Power - Rapid flash - module failure, contact Lutron
Hi Temp - Off - normal
Hi Temp - 1 sec on 7 sec off - module too hot, scaled to 25% power
Hi Temp - Continuous On - too hot, loads turned off
Hi Temp - Flashing 1 blink/sec - module was overheated and has now cooled.  to clear error press the input, prog, up, and down buttons simultaneously
Prog - Off - normal
Prog - Flashing - Device in program mode
Zones - Off - normal, zone off
Zones - On - Normal, zone on
Zones - 1 blink, pause - zone selected for manual control OR output shorted
Zones - 2 blink - overvoltage
Zones - 3 blink - shorted component
Zones - 4 blink - over temp, zone may be overloaded, scaled to 25%
Zones - 5 blink - over temp, turned off 
Zones - rapid flash - multiple errors -- shut the power down, disconnect, re-energize, connect the outputs one at a time to check errors
CCI - on - normal
CCI - rapid flash - emergency mode




QSN-4T5-120-D - 0-10V / Switching Module
---
4 zone module for 0-10/switching

fed with a single circuit -- single feed comes into the module -- the 4 outputs SHARE that one feed

cannot exceed 16 Amps lighting load for the entire module


each output rated for 50mA per output for 0-10 --- source or sink (sync?) - they can either provide or control

rule of thumb is 2mA per driver


the 0-10V fixtures must support switching power to turn on/off

all products have power failure memory -- if power is lost,  all relays stay in the last known position




QSN-4T20-D - 0-10V/Switching Module (independent feeds)
---

Each output is fed by its OWN INDEPENDENT FEED
16 A switching per output for lighting loads
20 A per output for AC general use / receptacles 



QSN-4S8-120-D - Switching Power Module
---

Single feed - 4 switched outputs

QSN-4S20-D - Switching Power Module (independent feeds)
---
independent feeds - 4 switched outputs

similar specs to the 4T20-D


QSN-1DALUNV-D / QSN-2DALUNV-D -- DALI Universal
---
provides 1 or 2 DALI buses depending on model
DALI = Digitally Addressable Lighting Interface

64 DALI addressable loads per bus

QSN-2ECO-120-D -- EcoSystem Power Module
---
Provides EcoSystem loop power for two loops

Each loop supports up to 64 devices (drivers) -- up to 128 devices per ESN

automatic replacement of a single failed ballast or driver

POLARITY INSENSITIVE

topology-free

the module DOES NOT provide power to the fixtures -- they get their power from another source 

RED LED MAY INDICATE LINE VOLTAGE ON THE ECO LINK


DIN module Wiring
---
Why are there 2 mains inputs on the phase adaptive module? --- for the contactor to temporarily make a connect to verify loads before you attach them to the module -- so you dont have to splice the hot coming in

Zone selector buttons allow you to verify the outputs -- plus many other functions

QS-Link wire harnesses available to pre-wire the panels
Wire landing board (QS-Link) - comes with the panel -- 5 terminals - run in parallel - good place to splice the QS-Link --- extra terminal #5 used for the DRAIN wire
DPM Line Voltage Wire Harness - power wiring harnesses -- module type specific, uses different harnesses for different module types
Terminal Block Kits -- switch from a single feed to a multi feed for QSN-4T20 / 4S20


each module counts as 1 device on the QS link device limit
each OUTPUT of each module counts as a switchleg -- ONLY if they are being used
ECO and DALI -- modules count as 1 QS device -- have to count how many devices are on the bus for the number of switchlegs

2000 feet link length limit (cable dependent)

you can add / field modify to add additional modules on the DIN panels
you CANNOT field modify the power harness / load center (breakers)
