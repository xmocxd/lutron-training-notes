--- Athena Wireless Node (AWN) - INSIDE THE FIXTURE  ---

communicated **TO** over wireless BY the processor, it relays information either 0-10 or DALI dimming information TO THE DRIVER

about the size of a quarter - goes in a fixture (via mfg)
comes with or without a sensor 

4 terminals -- can be used for DALI or 0-10 (automatically determines)
1 - DALI (DA -- dali data connection) - 0-10 (AUX+ -- this is AUXILLARY POWER provided TO the AWN to power it and keep it alive)
2 - DALI (DA) - 0-10 (AUX-)
3 - DALI (N/C -- no connection) - 0-10 (SIG+ --- this is the 0-10V control voltage SIGNAL translated from the digital signal provided out to the driver)
4 - DALI (N/C) - 0-10 (SIG- / DGND -- digital ground)

D4I -- ADDITIONAL dali subset -- allows the driver to supply power on the DALI bus/wiresz
DT6 / DT8 -- does not provide any digital voltage from the driver -- expect power from the controller
something can be designated DT8 + D4I for example***

if compatibility issue the power can be TURNED OFF on the DRIVER


if the driver DOES NOT have a built in power supply (for the AWN), lutron provides/sells a power supply (DFC-OEM-DBI)
